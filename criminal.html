This law replaced the Indian Penal Code, 1860. It streamlined the code to consist of only 358 sections, as opposed to the 511 sections in the IPC, 1860. Notable changes include the consolidation of offences against women and children under Chapter-V, and the introduction of ‘Community Service’ as a punishment for certain petty offences

This law prohibits a person from producing/manufacturing/cultivating, selling, purchasing, transporting, storing, and/or consuming any narcotic drug or psychotropic substance

This law makes it an offence for a public servant to take gratification other than legal remuneration in respect of an official act.

This law introduces a new offence for having sexual intercourse on false promise of marriage, employment, promotion, or by suppressing identity. It also introduces the offence of ‘snatching’, which was not present in the IPC, 1860. For the first time, ‘Community Service’ has been introduced as one of the punishments in Section 4 of the BNSS, 2023.

This law provides protection to children from offences of sexual assault, sexual harassment, and pornography.
